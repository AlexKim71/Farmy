Your online butcher with the largest range of meat in Switzerland

Straight from the animal and from the farm around the corner – that’s how we like our meat best. There is no room for compromise with our meat. Only the best and freshest pieces should be delivered to our customers. For this reason, the most important rules are: Transparency, animal well-being and a regional approach. We know exactly where and how our animals grow up and under which label our meat is certified.

We offer the largest online selection of meat in Switzerland: from nose-to-tail to fillet, from beef, pork, veal or lamb. The different labels prove us right: a large part of our products is certified by Bio Suisse Knospe or KAG Freiland. At the same time, our assortment is continuously expanding. We have the right meat for you, from the barbecue season and fondue chinoise to various specialities from all over Switzerland. Have fun visiting our online butchery.

Organic pork from the butcher

Although the colour of pork is lighter, it is still red meat. It contains myoglobin, a protein that gives it its red colour. But you can’t just use chops and ribs – almost all parts of the pig are usable and can be cooked. Pork is an important source of vitamin B1 and contains high amounts of zinc and selenium.

Poultry from Switzerland

Organic lamb from Switzerland

The main difference between mutton and the notorious lamb chops is the age of the sheep. Lamb comes from a young sheep under one year old, while mutton comes from an adult sheep. Lamb tends to be more expensive due to its benefits and taste profile. While the omega-6 and omega-3 ratio in lamb is low, it has a high content of minerals and vitamin B12.

The meat 101 with Farmy

We answer common questions about meat. For example, what is the correct term for separating tendons and bones from flesh? Right, it’s called parrying. Parrying involves removing tendons and bones from the meat before roasting.

How do you recognize high quality meat?

The meat should not lose too much liquid during roasting and its size should remain about the same. Another characteristic is the colour and consistency of the meat:

Tip 1:

Smell it! If the meat smells neutral (or even fine) – great! It smells rather sweet: better not to eat!

Tip 2:

A greenish colour is a sure sign that the meat is no longer fresh (unless, of course, it is supposed to look green – for example because of a marinade!)
